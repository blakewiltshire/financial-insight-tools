#### Watchlist, Trade Structuring & Risk Planning

The **Watchlist, Trade Structuring & Risk Planning** module supports the progression from candidate assets and strategic ideas through to structured planning scenarios and risk review.

Rather than prescribing strategies, generating signals, or simulating execution, the module provides a structured environment for examining potential trade configurations using historical asset data, user-defined assumptions, position-sizing logic, cost estimates, and capital-based risk parameters.

The module currently supports non-leveraged shares, leveraged CFD and spread-betting scenarios, and selected pairs or multi-leg relative structures. Futures, options and other specialised instruments are examined within the supporting guides but are not currently modelled as dedicated planning workflows.

Typical applications include:

- Maintaining external watchlists, candidate assets, and strategic ideas
- Structuring non-leveraged share scenarios
- Examining leveraged long or short CFD and spread-betting scenarios
- Exploring pairs and multi-leg relative structures
- Reviewing planned entry, stop, target, exposure, margin, and reward-to-risk assumptions
- Estimating known and assumed costs, including broker fees, spreads, transaction costs, slippage, and carrying costs where applicable
- Recording setup conditions, historical context, macro considerations, and planning notes
- Consolidating planning scenarios within an editable session-based dashboard
- Preserving reasoning through the wider observation and investigation workflow

#### Key Functions

- **Watchlist & Journal Editor**: Supports the import, review, amendment, and download of external watchlists, idea trackers, or trade journals
- **Trade Planning Worksheets**: Provides dedicated planning logic for non-leveraged shares, leveraged CFD and spread-betting exposure, and pairs or multi-leg structures
- **Capital & Exposure Planning**: Examines allocated capital, position value, notional exposure, margin requirements, leverage, and capital at risk
- **Risk & Reward Structuring**: Records user-defined stop distances, target distances, planned boundaries, and reward-to-risk assumptions
- **Cost & Currency Considerations**: Incorporates user-defined assumptions for broker fees, spreads, transaction costs, slippage, carrying costs, dividends, tax context, and currency conversion
- **Relative-Structure Context**: Supports manual review of spread relationships, volatility, correlation, Z-scores, support, resistance, and observed drift
- **Interactive Planning Dashboard**: Captures, edits, compares, and exports structured planning scenarios
- **Observation Integration**: Preserves contextual reasoning for use within the wider FIT investigation workflow

#### Scope

This module is a planning and review environment. It does not connect to brokers, execute trades, generate trading signals, predict market outcomes, or recommend investments.

All prices, costs, margin requirements, leverage assumptions, risk boundaries, targets, and scenario inputs should be reviewed against current market conditions and the relevant broker or execution platform before any independent decision is made.

Interpretation remains human. Execution decisions remain human.

#### ⚠️ No investment advice or recommendations are provided

The module supports structured planning, scenario examination, and preserved reasoning. It does not determine whether a trade should be placed or identify a preferred strategy, instrument, asset, entry, or exit.
